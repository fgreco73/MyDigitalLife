Quoridor

Quoridor is an abstract board game published in 1997 by Gigamic. The game was invented in 1995 by Mirko Marchesi under the original name Pinko Pallino and was later renamed Quoridor after changes were made to the board dimensions and the number of barriers.

In 2015, an association of Italian players was founded with the aim of promoting the spread of Quoridor in Italy: the WQO Italian Division, which published a tutorial on game strategies and tactics on its website.

Game rules
(Source: Wikipedia)
  • Each player starts from the center of their perimeter line, and the objective is to move their pawn to the opposite perimeter line. The first player to achieve this goal wins.
  • On their turn, a player can choose to move their pawn horizontally or vertically, or place a wall. A wall cannot be jumped over and must be moved around.
  • If two pawns are face to face, the player whose turn it is can jump over the opponent and land behind them. It is not possible to “block” a player completely using walls; they must always be left with a possible path to reach the goal.
